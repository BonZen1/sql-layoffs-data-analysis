# Key Insights: Global Layoffs Study

## Overview

This analysis looks at job cuts around the world. We studied layoffs by companies, industries, and countries to see patterns and trends.

## Key Findings

### 1. A few companies caused most layoffs

Some big tech companies had a lot of layoffs. This shows that big companies felt more pressure from the economy.

### 2. Some industries were hit harder

The tech industry had the most layoffs. Other industries also had layoffs, but not as many.

### 3. Layoffs were mostly in certain countries

Most layoffs happened in countries where big tech companies are located. This shows that industry trends, not local economies, caused layoffs.

### 4. Layoffs went up in certain years

Some years had a lot more layoffs than others. This shows how the economy affects jobs.

### 5. Layoffs happened in waves

Layoffs did not happen evenly every month. They came in waves, often after funding rounds, earnings reports, or company changes.

### 6. Cumulative layoffs show the scale

Looking at total layoffs over time shows how quickly job cuts can grow once they start.

### 7. Top companies with layoffs change every year

The companies with the most layoffs are different each year. This shows that company decisions matter too, not just the economy.

## Conclusion

The data shows that layoffs:

*Happen mostly in certain industries
*Come in waves, not slowly over time
*Are caused by both economy and company choices

Understanding this can help companies, investors, and people see job trends better.
